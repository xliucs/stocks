Business Overview

Micron Technology is one of only three companies worldwide (alongside Samsung and SK Hynix) that manufacture DRAM and NAND flash memory at scale. Headquartered in Boise, Idaho, the company commands approximately 25% of the global DRAM market and 12% of NAND. Micron's products are the critical building blocks of modern computing: DRAM provides the working memory in every server, PC, and smartphone, while NAND stores data in SSDs, mobile devices, and data center storage arrays. The company's product portfolio spans consumer, mobile, enterprise, automotive, and industrial end markets, with data center now the fastest-growing segment driven by AI infrastructure buildout.

The transformative catalyst is HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) — specialized stacked DRAM used in AI accelerators like NVIDIA's H100/H200/B100 GPUs. Micron's HBM3E products are in mass production with strong customer pull. HBM carries significantly higher ASPs and margins than commodity DRAM, fundamentally improving Micron's earnings power. The company is also a beneficiary of CHIPS Act subsidies, with $6.1B in grants supporting new fab construction in Idaho and New York.

With HBM3E in mass production, DRAM pricing at multi-year highs, and margins expanding to levels never seen in the company's history (56% gross margin last quarter), Micron is executing on a structural transformation from commodity memory maker to AI infrastructure enabler. We lean toward the former — HBM supply is oligopolistic (3 producers), customer-committed with long lead times, and tied to AI capex that shows no signs of peaking. Tomorrow's earnings report will be a critical data point. The stock has priced in a strong quarter; the guidance for Q3 and full-year will determine whether the multiple re-rates higher.
