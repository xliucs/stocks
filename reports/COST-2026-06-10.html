🚀 Catalysts

  • Defensive rotation: In the June 4–10 fear tape (S&P -1.6% on 6/10), COST rose +1.5% — staples with pricing power are catching the rotation bid out of tech/semis. Beta 0.87.
  • Oil shock = traffic weapon: Gasoline +40% y/y makes Costco's below-market member gas the cheapest in most markets; Q3 already showed record gas volumes. Fill-ups drive trips; trips drive baskets and renewals.
  • Membership fee increase still compounding: ~¼ of Q3's +10.7% fee growth came from the 2024 US/CA fee hike, which rolls through renewals into FY27. Renewal rate up to 92.2% despite it.
  • Strong labor market supports spend: 172k May payrolls (~2x consensus) is a tailwind for member demand even as it pressures the multiple via rates.
  • Warehouse expansion: ~30 openings planned FY26, increasingly international (high-ROIC China/Asia clubs).
  • E-commerce +21% and Costco Logistics record big-and-bulky volumes — the underappreciated growth vector.
  • Monthly sales reports (first Wednesday) provide steady positive datapoints; July 4-week sales print is next.
  • Dividend +13% (April 2026, to $1.30/qtr) plus the periodic special-dividend option (last: $15 in Jan 2024).

⚠️ Risks

  • The multiple, period: 43.5x forward vs a 4.54% 10Y and ~43% December hike odds. Every 5x of multiple compression ≈ $113 of stock. This is a rates story, not a fundamentals story.
  • De-rate through time: Stock is -4% over 12 months while EPS grew ~15% — holders are already paying the compression tax. If rates stay higher-for-longer, 38x (-13%) is a plausible floor multiple.
  • Oil-shock margin pinch: Gas retailing is high-revenue/low-margin — record volumes inflate sales but dilute margin %; sustained $91 WTI also raises logistics and food-input costs that Costco absorbs before repricing.
  • Consumer discretionary mix: Big-ticket non-foods (electronics, furniture, jewelry) are the first casualty if gasoline at +40% y/y squeezes household budgets.
  • Hard comps: FY25's gold/bullion and inflation-aided comps make FY27 comparisons tougher; comp deceleration below ~5% ex-gas would challenge the multiple.
  • WMT convergence: Walmart at 36.7x with +21% e-commerce growth of its own narrows COST's relative-quality argument.
  • No near-term catalyst: next earnings ~Sept 24; the stock trades on macro until then.
