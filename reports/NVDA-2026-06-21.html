⚠️ Risks

The new H200 approval is a partial, reversible thaw — not a restoration — and Beijing is actively pushing domestic substitutes. This is the single largest policy risk to the thesis.
  • AI capital-cycle financing (the new #1 market worry): NVDA's revenue is funded by customers now issuing >$400B of debt at rising rates and via circular vendor financing (the OpenAI/CoreWeave/Nebius web). If the financing window narrows, orders stretch right even though demand is real. A timing risk to FY28+ estimates, not a demand-existence risk.

  • Purchase-commitment leverage: ~$95.2B near-doubled supply commitments (~$119B total obligations) — a powerful weapon in shortage, a write-down generator if capex flattens (the 2018 crypto-hangover playbook).

🎯 Investment Thesis

NVIDIA at $211 is the same company it was at $237 in May — what changed first was the market's grading rubric, and now the macro is changing back. The early-June semis crash was a rate-hike scare amplified by AI-financing anxiety (circular vendor financing, >$400B of hyperscaler debt issuance, NVDA's own $25B note). By the week of June 15–21, the worst-case faded: the Strait of Hormuz reopened, WTI fell to ~$77, VIX collapsed to 16, and the Fed delivered a feared-but-priced hawkish hold under new Chair Warsh. NVDA reclaimed its 50-day and bounced 5% off the June low.

Against the noise: 85% trailing growth, 63% net margins, ~$40B net cash, Blackwell sold out, Q2 FY27 guided to ~$91B, GB300 supply tighter than any analyst has seen since Hopper, and Rubin's 10x inference-cost reduction landing into H2 2026 — at 16.6x FY28 earnings (23.5x current-year), the cheapest forward multiple in the company's modern history. Our own retrospective shows we systematically over-faded AI-compute winners on valuation anchoring; we won't repeat that. But two real risks keep this a Bullish-not-euphoric call: China DC-GPU sales remain banned at baseline (the H200 thaw is partial and reversible, with Beijing pushing domestic chips), and the entire cycle now leans on credit markets the Fed is no longer easing into.

The 12-month balance: demand is contracted reality, the financing/policy anxieties are timing-and-tail risks, and the multiple already prices meaningful deceleration. Bear $149 (-29%) needs a Fed hike and a capex air-pocket; base $280 (+33%) needs only estimates to hold while macro keeps calming; bull $392 (+86%) needs Rubin + a real China thaw + cuts. The enrich score of 70/100 (Bullish) reflects exactly this: cheap, dominant, and growing, but with an elevated policy overhang capping the upside conviction.
