What has changed since June is the backdrop, not the company: the stock has quietly de-rated ~15% from its May $1,096 record to $935, slipping below both its 50- and 200-day averages, while the S&P printed all-time highs in its best quarter in six years. That risk-on, record-high tape is precisely the regime a ~0.87-beta staple at 41x forward lags — and the Street has begun to act, with a fresh COST downgrade among late-June top calls. The one argument against the stock is the one that always existed — ~41x forward earnings against a 4.45% 10Y and a Fed debating a hike — but the slide is the news: that 41x is now a shade below the 5-year average rather than a premium to it, so the mean-reversion case (~$950) sits just above spot and the valuation entry is the most reasonable in over a quarter. DCF still can't get close, as it never has for a decade of outperformance.

Verdict: high-quality defensive hold / accumulate-on-weakness — 12-month base case $1,030 (+10%), with the $900–920 zone the attractive add level for a name that almost never gets cheap. Size for a $780 bear tail in a hike-and-rotation scenario. Expect COST to lag this record-high, risk-on tape and to outperform sharply if/when the Fed or a growth scare flips the market back toward defensives.
