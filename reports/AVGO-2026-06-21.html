🎯 Investment Thesis

Broadcom delivered the strongest quarter in its history, briefly suffered its worst session since IPO, and has now fully recovered — that round-trip is the whole story. Q2 FY26: revenue +48% to $22.2B, AI semis +143% to $10.8B, adjusted EPS +54% to $2.44, and Q3 guided to ~$29.4B (+84%) with AI ~$16B (+200%). The June 4 -12.6% crash was a positioning unwind on Hock Tan reaffirming (not raising) the FY27 ~$100B AI framework in a hike-fearing tape — not a fundamental break. Three weeks later the stock is back to $411, the sell-side raised numbers through the drop (BofA $530, Jefferies $550, JPM ~$580), and JPM is telling clients to be "aggressive buyers."

At 21.2x forward — below its own 5-year average and at a ~33% discount to AI-design peers — AVGO offers the highest-margin (76% gross), most contracted way to own the AI accelerator buildout: named multi-year XPU customers (Google, Meta, OpenAI, Anthropic, ByteDance, Fujitsu), an Ethernet-for-AI networking franchise, and a ~$29B/yr 90%-margin VMware annuity under it all. BofA's "AI Big 10" inclusion marks the institutional migration from semi-cyclical to core-AI-megacap. The bear case is real but narrower than the dip implied: XPU revenue is lumpy and concentrated in customers whose own financing is the cycle's pressure point, HBM cost inflation is rising, and the Fed's hawkish tilt can compress the multiple via rotation.

Policy risk is none — no pending export action touches the custom-ASIC franchise; ByteDance is a monitored tail, not a base case. Twelve-month skew: bear $300 (-27%) requires actual deployment delays plus a hike, base $465 (+13%) requires only a normal Sep 3 print holding the recovered multiple, bull $582 (+41%) requires the framework raise plus a Fed pivot.
