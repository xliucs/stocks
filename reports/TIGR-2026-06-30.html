🚨 The CSRC Crackdown — What Is Actually Affected

Facts as of June 30, 2026 (all sourced):
  • May 22: CSRC Beijing Bureau penalized UP Fintech subsidiaries for unlicensed cross-border securities/fund/futures business in mainland China: fine RMB308.1M (~$45M) + confiscation of RMB103.1M (~$15M) illegal income = RMB411M (~$57–60M) total; CEO Tianhua Wu personally warned and fined RMB1.25M (Bloomberg; SCMP; FX News Group; StockTitan 6-K, May 22). ADRs fell as much as 47% pre-market, closed −25–28%.
  • Cohort-wide: the same campaign hit Futu (~RMB1.85B / ~$273M) and Longbridge. Regulators vowed to "fully eradicate" non-compliant cross-border brokerage within two years (SCIO/China Services Info, May 25–26; LeapRate).
  • June 12 (now in force): Tiger suspended new account openings and position additions for all existing mainland China investor accounts — the two-year, sell-only rectification is live, not prospective (@CHItrader, June 2 notice; StockTitan). Existing mainland accounts may only sell and withdraw; mainland infrastructure must ultimately be dismantled.
  • Exposure: mainland China retail client assets ≈ 10% of total client assets (~$5.9B of $58.9B); management frames direct mainland revenue exposure at ~0.6%. The larger risk is second-order — growth optics, compliance cost, and scrutiny of overseas-Chinese acquisition channels.
  • Competitive read: HSBC is reported to be stepping into the void TIGR/FUTU are vacating for mainland global-market demand (@BrianTycangco) — i.e., the wind-down may be a permanent share donation to licensed incumbents, not a parked opportunity.
  • No new CSRC action since: as of quarter-end there is no defined final boundary to the campaign — the open-ended two-year mandate keeps this a live overhang through 2028. That indeterminacy, not any single fresh headline, is what the multiple keeps discounting.
