🎯 Investment Thesis

TSMC just printed +30.1% May revenue growth on the same day the market refused to buy good news — that is the whole setup. Operationally, this is the strongest position in the company's history: >30% FY26 USD growth guided and tracking, 66% gross margins, CoWoS-L sold out, customers pre-booking wafers into 2027, and 3nm price hikes of up to 15% coming in H2. TSM is the toll booth on every lane of the AI highway — NVDA GPUs, AVGO XPUs, AMD accelerators all converge on the same fabs — which is why it was the relative-strength winner of the June crash (-6% week vs -22% for AVGO).

The bear case has two distinct legs. Cyclical: 26% of revenue faces IDC's -13% smartphone year (a 10-year low) and the capex supercycle suppresses FCF exactly when the market began punishing capital intensity. Geopolitical: the Iran strikes and Hormuz blockade reminded markets that tails get repriced suddenly, and TSM's Taiwan tail is the largest in global equities — our DCF charges ~1.5pp of extra WACC for it and still can't make the stock cheap. After a +100% year, the valuation argument has shifted from re-rating to earnings compounding: at 20.9x forward, TSM now costs more than its two biggest customers.

Twelve-month balance: bear $299 (-27%) needs a consumer collapse plus escalation; base $449 (+10%) needs only delivery of guided numbers; bull $538 (+32%) needs price-hike margin flow-through and a calmer world. The asymmetry is mildly positive but the leanest of our four semis — appropriate for the highest-quality, most-owned, and geopolitically heaviest name in the set.
