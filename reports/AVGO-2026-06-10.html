🎯 Investment Thesis

Broadcom just delivered the strongest quarter in its history and suffered its worst one-day loss since going public — that contradiction is the whole investment question. Q2 FY26: revenue +48%, AI semis +143% to $10.8B, adjusted EPS +54%, and over $30B of new AI orders booked. The -14% June 4 crash (~$280B) was triggered not by any of that but by a Q3 AI guide of ~$16B versus a $17.2B whisper and Hock Tan declining to raise the FY27 ~$100B AI framework. In the new macro regime — December hike odds ~43%, ORCL punished for capex, SMCI for dilution — "merely meeting the plan" is a sell signal. AVGO was the proof.

The bull case is that nothing fundamental broke: backlog grew ~3x faster than revenue, BofA raised estimates and its PT to $530 after the print, the customer list (Google, Meta, OpenAI, Anthropic, ByteDance, Fujitsu, FuriosaAI incoming) is multi-year contracted, and the stock now trades at 19.2x forward — a 39% discount to AI peers and below its own 5-year average — with a $29B/yr software annuity under it. The bear case is real but narrower: XPU revenue is lumpy and concentrated in customers whose own financing is the cycle's pressure point; if OpenAI's raise stalls or Google digests TPU capacity, the FY27 framework back-loads and the guidance-cadence punishment repeats.

We set policy risk at none (no pending export action touches the custom-ASIC franchise; ByteDance is a monitored tail, not a base case).
