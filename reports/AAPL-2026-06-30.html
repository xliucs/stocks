🎯 Investment Thesis

AAPL is still the right stock for a capital-intensity-averse regime at a price that already knows it — but the June 25 memory-driven price hikes added a genuine, near-term margin cloud that wasn't there a week ago. Apple is the structural winner of the anti-capex tape — $101B FCF after just ~$39B capex, zero dilution, a ~$110B buyback — and at WWDC it doubled down on asset-light AI by renting a custom Google Gemini (~$1B/yr, on Nvidia/Google Cloud) to finally ship a working Siri. But it sits on the cost side of the DRAM/HBM supercycle now leading the market: DRAM contract prices ~+98% in Q1 forced $100–300 hikes on Mac and iPad, drove the worst single day since April 2025, and put the 49% gross-margin high squarely in play into the July 30 print. Layer on IDC's −13% smartphone forecast against a 42%-iPhone revenue mix, a Siri shipped in beta on rented intelligence with no GA date, the Cook→Ternus CEO handoff plus OpenAI poaching the Vision Pro chief, ~$100M of insider selling with zero buys, and three of four valuation methods at or below spot — and the risk/reward is asymmetric to the downside near-term. The bull case — installed-base forced upgrades, Services compounding, Apple–Intel onshoring, and memory-supply lock-in — is real but needs evidence the multiple already assumes.
