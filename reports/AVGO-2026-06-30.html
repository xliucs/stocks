🎯 Investment Thesis

Broadcom's fundamentals kept improving through June even as its stock did not. Q2 FY26 was the strongest quarter in its history — revenue +48% to $22.2B, AI semis +143% to $10.8B, adjusted EPS +54% to $2.44 — and on June 24 OpenAI unveiled "Jalapeño," its first custom inference ASIC, built by Broadcom in a record ~9-month cycle with gigawatt-scale deployment via Microsoft starting end-2026. That is the biggest new design-win headline of the year. Yet the stock, having round-tripped its June 4 crash back to ~$411 on June 21, slipped to $377.75 into June 30 as the Nasdaq fell into correction, the Fed dot plot turned hike-leaning, and the Strait-of-Hormuz oil shock drove a rotation out of long-duration tech into energy/cyclicals.

At 19.5x forward — near the low end of its multi-year range and a ~38% discount to AI-design peers — AVGO offers the highest-margin (76% gross), most contracted way to own the AI accelerator buildout: named multi-year XPU customers (Google, Meta, OpenAI, Anthropic, ByteDance, Fujitsu), a renewed 5-year Alphabet TPU pact, ~$30B of AI bookings against $10.8B shipped, an Ethernet-for-AI networking franchise, and a ~$29B/yr 90%-margin VMware annuity under it all. The bear case is real but macro-shaped: XPU revenue is lumpy and concentrated in customers whose own financing is the cycle's pressure point, HBM cost inflation is rising, TPU v9 timing is debated, and a hawkish Fed plus the Hormuz shock can compress the multiple via rotation regardless of execution.

Policy risk is none — no pending export action touches the custom-ASIC franchise; ByteDance is a monitored tail, not a base case. Twelve-month skew: bear $300 (-21%) requires actual deployment delays plus a hike, base $465 (+23%) requires only a normal Sep 3 print and a stabilizing macro, bull $582 (+54%) requires the framework raise plus a Fed pivot. Technically the $360 200-day / $365 recent-low zone is the line that keeps the uptrend intact.

Verdict: Accumulate on weakness. 12-month base case $465 (+23%), bull $582 (+54%) if the FY27 framework is raised and Jalapeño ramps; bear $300 (-21%) in a deployment-delay-plus-hike tail. Key catalyst: Sep 3 Q3 FY26 print. Key monitor: the rate/rotation regime and hyperscaler/OpenAI financing — they are moving this stock more than its own numbers right now.
