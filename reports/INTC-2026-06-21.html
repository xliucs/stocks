Bear: 86.6x forward earnings, 12.5x sales, negative GAAP EPS and -$8.3B FCF — at a record high, in a regime that specifically punishes capital intensity, under a Fed that just signaled a possible hike. The two catalysts behind the +21% month are reported, not confirmed (JPMorgan: Google may be packaging-only; WSJ: Apple is low-end 18A-P only, TSMC keeps >90%). External foundry revenue is $174M of $5.4B; the Google order recognizes in 2028; consensus PT ($94) is 30% below spot; and the crowd is openly chasing "$160 by Friday" after +606% off the low.

Verdict: Hold, tilting toward Trim — respect the turnaround, but the record high on un-confirmed headlines is where to take chips off, not add. Our methods cluster fair value at $85–100 (-25 to -37%), and even the duopoly-bull PV is only +4%. We hold this with the same humility that kept us wrong from $60 to $134 — the foundry news flow has repeatedly converted bear probability into bull probability. But the asymmetry has flipped: a year ago the market underpriced the option; today, at the 52-week high with consensus 30% below and reward-to-risk inverted, the stock prices a near-certain bull outcome on deals that companies have not confirmed. For new money: wait for the $100–110 accumulation zone (upgraded-consensus/breakout confluence) rather than chase the high; existing holders ride with a trailing stop and consider trimming into strength. The July 23 print, an Apple/Google confirmation-or-denial, and any NVDA-18A formalization are the swing events.
