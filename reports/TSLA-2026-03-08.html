  • Robotaxi / Cybercab Launch (Mid-2026): Tesla autonomous ride-hailing network could transform unit economics and TAM from capex-heavy vehicle sales to high-margin software/services.
  • Full Self-Driving (FSD) Monetization (2026-2027): Subscription revenue and potential OEM licensing represents a high-margin recurring stream. V13+ achieving safety milestones is critical.
  • Optimus Humanoid Robot Production (2026-2028): Musk targets 1M+ units/year by 2030. Internal deployment at Gigafactories first, then commercial rollout. Potential TAM in the hundreds of billions.
  • Energy Storage Hypergrowth: Megapack deployments accelerating with massive grid-scale demand. Energy segment grew 67% YoY and is Teslas highest-margin business.

  • Affordable New Model (~$25K, H1 2026): Cheaper vehicle designed without traditional controls, enabling autonomy-first design and opening mass market to first-time EV buyers.

  • Elon Brand Damage: DOGE involvement and controversial political statements led to Tesla boycotts in Europe and parts of US, with measurable delivery volume declines. Brand recovery timeline uncertain.
  • Core Auto Business Deterioration: FY2025 revenue -3.1% YoY to $94.8B. Gross margins compressed to 18% from 25%+ peak. Operating margin hit 2.6% in Q1 2025 before recovering slightly.
  • Extreme Valuation Premium: At 141x forward PE and $1.5T market cap, perfect execution on multiple unproven product lines is required. DCF on current FCF yields $17/share intrinsic value.

  • Optimus Execution Risk: Humanoid robotics unproven at scale. Technical challenges and competition from Boston Dynamics, Figure AI, and 1X could delay commercial viability.
  • Heavy Capital Allocation Burden: Capex >20B in 2026 vs $3.7B FCF creates significant pressure. Tesla must demonstrate clear ROI on AI infrastructure, Supercharger, and manufacturing investments.
