🚨 The CSRC Crackdown — What Is Actually Affected

Facts as of June 10, 2026 (all sourced):
  • May 22: CSRC Beijing Bureau notified UP Fintech subsidiaries of penalties for unlicensed cross-border securities/fund/futures business in mainland China: fine of RMB308.1M (~$45M) plus confiscation of RMB103.1M (~$15M) "illegal income" — ~$60M total; CEO Tianhua Wu personally warned and fined RMB1.25M (TIGR 6-K via StockTitan; FX News Group; Bloomberg, May 22). Stock fell ~23–29% on the news (Seeking Alpha; Simply Wall St).
  • Scope: The same campaign penalized Futu and Longbridge. Regulators vow to "fully eradicate" non-compliant cross-border brokerage within two years (SCIO, May 25; LeapRate).
  • Exposure quantified: Mainland-China retail client assets ≈ 10% of total client assets as of end-2025 (company disclosure in 6-K) — i.e., ~$5.9B of the $58.9B. 36Kr reports ~1M mainland accounts across the affected brokers entering a "two-year countdown."
  • Transition rules: existing mainland accounts may ONLY sell and withdraw — no new purchases, no new deposits, no new mainland account openings. Attrition of this book is mandated, not hypothetical.
  • Our May 27 report understated this: we cited "mainland ~0.6% of FY25 revenue" — the relevant disclosed metric is now ~10% of client assets, and the second-order effects (growth-rate hit, compliance costs, headline risk to overseas-Chinese clients) are larger than the direct revenue loss.
