🎯 Investment Thesis

BABA is two companies: a genuinely accelerating AI/cloud platform (+38% cloud growth, AI ~30% of external cloud and triple-digit growth for 11+ straight quarters, the Qwen model family) bolted onto a barely-growing consumption business that just spent its way to a ~−62% non-GAAP profit decline and the first FCF-negative year in company history. At $107.10 and 11.6x forward the stock screens cheap and sits on a >$75B net-cash fortress — but the ~20% June drop was not just macro: the June 8 Pentagon "Chinese Military Company" (1260H) designation added a discrete sanctions tail on top of a hawkish FOMC, China's July-1 outbound-investment rules, and a soft 618 festival (+3.2%). Within its own cohort PDD and JD trade at ~half the multiple, and our own backtests show BABA has repeatedly lagged QQQ on exactly this "cheap" thesis. The catalysts that close the gap are specific and dated — quick-commerce breakeven by end FY3/27, AI crossing 50% of cloud revenue, and a 1260H resolution — all 2–4 quarters away, while RSI 22.7 and a test of the $103.71 52-week low define a falling-knife tape. Verdict: deeply oversold and fundamentally interesting, but this is patience over urgency — 12-month base case $126 (+18%), bull $180 if cloud and a 1260H reprieve land; size for a $78 bear tail in a sanctions-escalation scenario.
