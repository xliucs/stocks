🎯 Investment Thesis

NVIDIA at $200 is the same company it was at $237 two weeks ago — what changed is the market's grading rubric. The June 4–5 semis crash (NVDA -6%, then a slide back below $5T) was triggered by a rate-hike scare and amplified by a new fear: that the AI buildout's financing — ORCL's $40B raise, SMCI's dilution, leveraged neocloud debt — is the cycle's weak link. NVDA's $95.2B of supply commitments and $119B purchase obligations make it structurally long that financing cycle.

Against that: 85% trailing growth, 63% net margins, ~$40B net cash, Blackwell sold out, Q2 FY27 guided to ~$91B, and Rubin's 10x inference-token-cost reduction landing into H2 2026 — at 15.7x forward earnings, the cheapest in the company's modern history. Our own retrospective shows we systematically over-faded AI-compute winners on valuation anchoring (AMD, MU doubled against our caution); we won't repeat that. But we also won't momentum-chase into a regime that just demonstrated it can knock 6% off the best balance sheet in tech on a jobs print.

The 12-month balance: demand is contracted reality, the financing anxiety is a timing risk, and the multiple already prices meaningful deceleration. Bear $149 (-26%) requires a Fed hike and a capex air-pocket; base $280 (+40%) requires only that estimates hold and rate panic fades; bull $392 (+96%) needs Rubin + China + cuts. Asymmetry remains clearly positive — wider than in May, with a deeper bear tail than the old regime priced.
