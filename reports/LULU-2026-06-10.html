⚠️ Risks

  • Value-trap continuation (the base risk): three guidance cuts in 12 months; forward EPS estimates still falling. A 10x multiple on declining EPS produced -27% since April — it can do it again. UAA is the cautionary template.
  • Competitive structural share loss: Alo Yoga and Vuori opening stores in LULU's best malls; Skims expanding into adjacent categories; ONON/Hoka winning footwear-adjacent wallet. None of these are cyclical.
  • Oil-shock discretionary squeeze: gasoline +40% y/y hits exactly the $100+ apparel impulse purchase; the June macro regime is the worst possible backdrop for a premium-price apparel turnaround.
  • Brand-narrative spiral: management itself cites negative media/social commentary as a traffic driver — including coverage of the Texas AG (Paxton) PFAS investigation opened April 13, 2026 into "forever chemicals" claims (LULU says PFAS was phased out in FY23). Low direct financial risk, real brand-perception risk.
  • Tariff and input costs: Vietnam/Cambodia-heavy sourcing remains tariff-exposed; oil-linked synthetic-fabric (nylon/lycra) input costs rise with $91 WTI.
  • CEO-transition execution risk: a September outsider arrival means strategy limbo through H2; interim co-CEO structure rarely makes bold inventory/markdown decisions.
  • China concentration in the bull case: the only growth pillar is also the most geopolitically fragile one.

🎯 Investment Thesis

LULU is no longer a "premium brand at a cheap multiple" story — that thesis failed three times on the way from $180 to $119, and the June 4 print confirmed why: North America is guided down high-single-digits, EPS fell 35% YoY, and management itself admits the product engine and brand heat are broken while Alo, Vuori, and Skims take share. What remains is a genuinely strong cash machine (55.7% gross margin, 8.4% FCF yield, $1.5B cash, international +22%, China +20%, India launching) trading at 10.2x — with insider buying from the board chair and incoming-CEO catalyst (Heidi O'Neill, Sept 8) offering a credible path to a 13–14x re-rate. The macro regime (gasoline +40% y/y squeezing discretionary, defensive rotation away from consumer cyclicals) argues against paying up early.

Verdict: Neutral / wait-for-proof. The 12-month base case is $125 (+5%) — the asymmetry only turns attractive on either a flush toward $95–100 or the first hard evidence (Q2 print Sept 3, holiday slate) that the NA decline is decelerating. Do not repeat the knife-catch.
