⚠️ Risks

  • China export controls (policyRisk: ELEVATED — the structural overhang): A full data-center-GPU revenue segment to China remains banned at baseline. CFO Kress has confirmed China is excluded from guidance after the H20 ban; Q1 FY26 took a $4.5B inventory charge and forfeited ~$8B of H20 revenue. The new H200 approval is partial, conditional (25% revenue cut, ~50% volume cap, case-by-case) and reversible — not a restoration — and Beijing is actively pushing domestic substitutes. The ~$50B 2028 China AI-accelerator TAM is largely forgone and policy can tighten around Taiwan/Iran escalation. This is the single largest policy risk to the thesis.
  • AI capital-cycle financing (the market's #1 worry): NVDA's revenue is funded by customers issuing hundreds of billions of debt at rising rates and via circular vendor financing (the OpenAI/CoreWeave/Nebius web). If the financing window narrows, orders stretch right even though demand is real — a timing risk to FY28+ estimates, not a demand-existence risk.
  • Rate regime: The Fed's hawkish June dot plot (9/19 see a 2026 hike, easing bias removed) and 10Y near 4.5% cap multiple expansion. At beta ~2.2, NVDA is the long-duration proxy of the whole market.
  • Custom ASIC substitution: TPU v7, Maia 200, Trainium 3, MTIA. Inference share projected to erode by 2028. Mitigants: training share intact, CUDA's ~5M developers, Rubin's ~5x inference gain.
  • Purchase-commitment leverage: ~$95B+ near-doubled supply commitments — a powerful weapon in shortage, a write-down generator if capex flattens (the 2018 crypto-hangover playbook).
  • Insider optics: Trailing transactions are all disposals/gifts; a $221M director sale on the June 4 break day; zero open-market buys at ~-15% off highs.
  • Geopolitics: Renewed US–Iran strikes (June 27–28) keep an oil/energy tail live; Taiwan single-source (TSMC CoWoS-L) remains the existential tail.

🎯 Investment Thesis

NVIDIA at $200 is the same company it was at $237 in May — what changed first was the market's grading rubric, and the macro is now stabilizing while the memory-supply leg just got de-risked. The early-June semis break was a rate-hike scare amplified by AI-financing anxiety. By quarter-end the picture had firmed: markets are closing the strongest quarter in ~six years, VIX sits ~17.6, the 10Y is back below 4.5%, the Fed's feared hawkish hold under new Chair Warsh is priced, and — critically — Micron's June 24 blowout (record $41.5B, ~86% GM guide, HBM4 sold out and contracted) hard-confirmed that Rubin-platform demand is real and pulling forward.

Against the noise: 85% trailing growth, 63% net margins, ~$40B net cash, Blackwell/GB300 sold out, Q2 FY27 guided to ~$92B, GB300 leading the first agentic-AI benchmark, and Rubin's ~5x inference gain landing in Q3 2026 — at 15.7x FY28 earnings (22.3x current-year), the cheapest forward multiple in the company's modern history and the cheapest of any AI-compute leader in its peer set. Strikingly, NVDA has lagged the June melt-up in MU, AMD, TSM and Samsung's HBM chain. Our own retrospective shows we systematically over-faded AI-compute winners on valuation anchoring; we won't repeat that. But two real risks keep this Bullish-not-euphoric: China DC-GPU sales remain banned at baseline (the H200 thaw is partial, conditional, and reversible, with Beijing pushing domestic chips), and the entire cycle leans on credit markets the Fed is no longer easing into.

The 12-month balance: demand is contracted reality, the financing/policy anxieties are timing-and-tail risks, and the multiple already prices meaningful deceleration. Bear $149 (-26%) needs a Fed hike and a capex air-pocket; base $281 (+40%) needs only estimates to hold while macro keeps calming; bull $392 (+96%) needs Rubin + a real China thaw + cuts.
