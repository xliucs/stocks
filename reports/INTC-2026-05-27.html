🎯 Investment Thesis

INTC is the cleanest example in semis of the market paying for an out-year option that hasn't priced in execution risk. The Q1 '26 print was genuinely good — sixth straight revenue beat, foundry pipeline at $15B+ with MSFT/AMZN signed, 18A yields rising 7%/month, $7.86B of CHIPS Act funding finalized. But at $121.77 and 79.1x forward earnings on a -5.9% net margin, the stock now trades at a multiple richer than AMD (38x at 38% revenue growth) and NVDA (17x at 85% growth), with every independent valuation method — peer parity ($41.95), 5y average ($43.12), Wall Street consensus PT ($87.76) — flagging a fair value well below spot. The bull math only works if you underwrite ~$3.50 of normalized EPS in 2028, which requires the foundry-pipeline-to-revenue conversion AND a 70% 18A yield AND no macro disruption to long-duration cash flows.
