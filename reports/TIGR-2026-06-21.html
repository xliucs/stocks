🚨 The CSRC Crackdown — What Is Actually Affected

Facts as of June 21, 2026 (all sourced):
  • May 22: CSRC Beijing Bureau penalized UP Fintech subsidiaries for unlicensed cross-border securities/fund/futures business in mainland China: fine RMB308.1M (~$45M) + confiscation of RMB103.1M (~$15M) "illegal income" = RMB411M (~$57–60M) total; CEO Tianhua Wu personally warned/fined (Bloomberg; SCMP; FX News Group, May 22). ADRs fell as much as 47% pre-market, closed −25–28% at $4.36.
  • Cohort-wide: the same campaign hit Futu (~RMB1.85B / ~$273M) and Longbridge. Regulators vowed to "fully eradicate" non-compliant cross-border brokerage within two years (SCIO, May 25; LeapRate). Citic flagged ~US$32B of HK assets caught in the wider sweep (SCMP).
  • June 12 (NEW since last report): Tiger formally suspended new account openings and position additions for all existing mainland China investor accounts — phased rectification is now live, not prospective (@CHItrader, June 2; transition-rule confirmation). Existing mainland accounts may only sell and withdraw.
  • Exposure: mainland China retail client assets ≈ 10% of total client assets (~$5.9B of $58.9B); ~1M mainland accounts cohort-wide entered a "two-year countdown" (36Kr). Management frames direct mainland revenue exposure as ~0.6% — but the second-order effects (growth optics, compliance cost, overseas-Chinese channel scrutiny) are larger than the direct line.
  • Competitive read (NEW): HSBC is reported to be stepping into the void TIGR/FUTU are vacating for mainland global-market demand (@BrianTycangco, June 17) — i.e., the wind-down may be a permanent share donation, not a parked opportunity.
