The bear facts are unusually concrete: ~57% of revenue is handsets in a year IDC calls -13% (a 10-year decade low), memory-price inflation squeezes its customers' BOMs, the QTL annuity decays with the same unit base, the Apple modem cliff is on schedule, and the Street's mean target ($180) sits below the price with a Hold rating — the worst consensus setup in our coverage. Our DCF agrees: on cash flows that exist today, fair value is ~$142–$160, not $191.

The bull facts are equally concrete: automotive +38% at a record $1.33B against a $45B pipeline, IoT growing, 17.9x forward / ~4.8% FCF yield / 1.9% dividend / $20B buyback, and two dated catalysts — the June 24 Investor Day and the December-quarter hyperscaler shipment — either of which could force the stale-below-price consensus to chase upward. Positioning is fully reset (P/C from 0.48 to ~1.0).

12-month base case: $181 (-5%) — the only name in our four where base case is below price. This is a catalyst-contingent story, not a value story: own it for June 24 and the December shipment with sized risk, or wait for either to print. Balanced verdict: the diversification is real, but until data-center revenue is booked, QCOM remains the most macro-exposed and policy-exposed (China ~46%) of our semis at a multiple that no longer screams cheap on clean numbers.
