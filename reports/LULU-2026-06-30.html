🎯 Investment Thesis

LULU is a fallen-angel value setup: a once-30x-multiple compounder now at 9.2x trailing / 10.0x forward earnings and 1.16x sales after a 53% one-year collapse, ~9% off a fresh 52-week low. The bull case is concrete — a 55.7% gross margin, 32% ROE, ~8.7% FCF yield, no meaningful net debt, +13% China comps, a Nike-veteran CEO (Heidi O'Neill) arriving Sept 8, and the rare signal of insiders buying on the way down (Director Charles Bergh ~$1.5M incl. post-guidance-cut; interim co-CEO ~$495K; zero open-market selling). Our DCF says that if cash flows merely stabilize, the stock is worth ~$184 — the market is pricing near-permanent decline. The bear case is equally concrete and is why the multiple compressed: Americas comps are −5% to −6% with ~8 quarters of trend deterioration, Alo/Vuori/Skims are taking share, tariffs cost ~$240–320M/yr, and management just cut FY26 EPS guidance to ~$11 — so 9–10x may be fair value for a structurally maturing brand, not a bargain. NKE's simultaneous China −17% / CFO-exit print is a live reminder that cheap apparel can get cheaper.

Verdict: Speculative value buy on a 12-month view — base case $132 (+16%) requires only stabilization, bull $180 (+58%) needs the O'Neill turnaround to take; size for a $88 bear tail (−23%) if US comps keep deteriorating or China cracks. The Sept 3 print and the new CEO's plan are the swing factors. This is a "is it a value trap?" bet — the insider buying, fortress balance sheet, and cash generation tilt the risk/reward favorably, but only for investors who can hold through continued US softness.
