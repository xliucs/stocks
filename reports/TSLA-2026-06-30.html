🎯 Investment Thesis

TSLA is the market's purest narrative-vs-numbers trade, and the late-June risk-on tape has handed the bulls a momentum reprieve — +8.5% on June 29 and +2.1% on June 30 to $420.60 — without resolving the core tension. The narrative is genuinely advancing: cumulative robotaxi paid miles are ramping, FSD hit ~1.28M subscribers (+51% y/y) across 10 countries, the proprietary paid ride-hailing service goes live this quarter, a first unsupervised Cybercab launch is in the air, and Musk's June 16 exercise of his 2018 package (one week into the SpaceX IPO) increased insider alignment. But the numbers remain the weakest in the group: a 4.2% operating margin, ~$5.25B FCF on a $1.58T cap (~301x), ~$25B+ of 2026 capex pushing FCF-after-capex toward zero, and a 168.1x forward multiple above the stock's own 5-year ceiling. The June 17 hawkish Fed (dot-plot flip, lifted PCE) plus a visible rotation out of high-multiple tech is precisely the macro that pressures a long-duration, no-earnings-floor profile, and no cash-flow scenario we can model reaches $420 (bull DCF ~$287). Michael Burry's fresh short at $416.22 crystallizes the bear case; the analyst cluster sits at a Hold near $421. The immediate swing factor is the July 2 Q2 delivery print against a ~406K bar.

Verdict: Hold / trim into strength on a 12-month view — base case $420 (±0%, fairly valued at consensus, dead money with violent swings), bear $212 (−50%) if a hawkish-Fed high-beta de-rating or delivery miss lands, bull $717 only on delivery-beat plus driverless-at-scale proof.
