🎯 Investment Thesis

NVIDIA presents a rare combination at a $5T market cap: 85% YoY revenue growth, 74% gross margins, 63% net margins, 114% ROE — and a 16.8x forward P/E. That multiple is the lowest in five years and below every peer in the megacap semi set (AMD 38x, AVGO 23x, TSM 22x). The market is pricing NVDA for severe deceleration (sub-30% growth) while company-guided Q2 revenue of $91B implies +56% YoY at the midpoint — and Blackwell is "sold out" by Jensen's own description.

The bear case isn't demand — it's substitution. Custom ASICs from Google (TPU v7), Microsoft (Maia 200), and AWS (Trainium 3) are real and growing at 44% CAGR, with NVDA's inference share potentially halving by 2028. But (a) training share remains nearly intact, (b) CUDA's 17-year accumulation of frameworks/libraries is irreplaceable on any 2–3 year horizon, and (c) the Vera Rubin platform launching H2 2026 (5x inference perf vs Blackwell) directly addresses the inference cost-per-token attack vector.

With ~$600B in 2026 hyperscaler capex (+36% YoY) flowing predominantly to NVDA, networking growing +267%, and the next upgrade cycle (Rubin) already de-risked via TSMC capacity pre-bookings, the asymmetry is heavily skewed up. Bear case (-13% to -25%) vs. base case (+49–64%) vs. bull case (+105–188%) — buying 85% growth at 17x with this risk distribution is a textbook fundamental mispricing.
