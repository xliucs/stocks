🚀 Catalysts

  • Dominant ICE/Truck Franchise: Full-size trucks and SUVs (Silverado, Sierra, Suburban, Tahoe) remain best-in-class and highly profitable. Vehicle sales rose 5.5% in 2025 to 2.85M units — volume growth while peers struggled.
  • Software & Services Flywheel: OnStar/Super Cruise deferred revenue hit $5.4B at end of 2025, up 65% YoY. This high-margin recurring revenue stream is growing into a significant profit driver.
  • EV Capacity Right-Sizing Complete: GM took one-time Q4 2025 charges to right-size EV production. Now positioned for sustainable, lower-loss EV scaling with the Ultium platform in 2026+.
  • Q1 2026 Earnings Catalyst (April 28): Consensus expects $2.57 EPS. GM has beaten estimates for 3+ consecutive quarters — an upside surprise could catalyze a re-rating.
  • Aggressive Buybacks Driving EPS: GM has been reducing share count rapidly, compounding EPS growth even in flat revenue environments. Forward EPS of ~$13.88 implies <6x P/E.
  • GM Financial Captive Finance: Recurring fee income and financing margin provide earnings stability and deepen customer loyalty across the vehicle lifecycle.

⚠️ Risks

  • Tariff Headwinds ($3-4B Impact): GM expects $3-4B gross tariff costs in 2026 from Canada/Mexico production exposure. ~750K vehicles were imported from these markets to the U.S. in 2024. Partial offset via USMCA credits, but net impact remains material.
  • EV Losses & Transition Costs: Q4 2025 net loss widened to -$3.3B due to EV capacity adjustment charges. EV segment continues to generate losses as GM scales — path to EV profitability remains uncertain.
  • High Debt Load ($131.6B): Total debt far exceeds cash ($27.7B), primarily from GM Financial. Significant leverage limits financial flexibility in a downturn or recession scenario.
  • EV Subsidy Removal: Trump administration halting EV tax credits reduces consumer demand for EVs, potentially slowing GM's electrification roadmap and increasing competitive disadvantage vs. Tesla.
  • Margin Compression: Gross margins at 10.7% are thin for an industrial company. Full-year EBIT fell 15% to $12.7B vs $14.9B in 2024 — sustained margin pressure would threaten earnings power.
  • China JV Deterioration: SAIC-GM faces intensifying competition from BYD and local Chinese EV brands. Equity income from China is declining and restructuring costs are mounting.
  • Cruise Autonomous Vehicle Uncertainty: Robotaxi program has faced major regulatory setbacks. Future path remains unclear with ongoing cash burn and reputational overhang.
