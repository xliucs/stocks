BABA is two companies: a genuinely accelerating AI/cloud platform (+38% cloud growth, AI ~30% of external cloud and triple-digit growth for 11+ straight quarters, the Qwen family) bolted onto a barely-growing consumption business that just spent its way to a ~−62% non-GAAP profit decline and the first FCF-negative year in company history. At $95.98 and 10.6x forward — now below Tencent — the stock screens cheap and sits on a net-cash balance sheet, but the June leg down was not macro alone: it is a two-headline shock. On June 23 Alibaba sued the Pentagon to escape the June 8 "Chinese Military Company" (1260H) designation (a genuine mitigant — Xiaomi and AMEC won similar suits), and on June 24 Anthropic accused Alibaba of "the largest known distillation attack" to train Qwen off Claude (denied, unverified) — a reputational/regulatory tail aimed squarely at the AI engine the bull case capitalizes. Within its own cohort PDD and JD still trade at ~60% of BABA's multiple, and our backtests show BABA has repeatedly lagged QQQ on exactly this "cheap" thesis. RSI at 5.2 and a fresh 52-week low ($91.99) define a full-capitulation, falling-knife tape. The catalysts that close the gap are specific and dated — quick-commerce breakeven by end FY3/27, AI crossing 50% of cloud revenue, and a 1260H court win — all 2–4 quarters away. Verdict: deeply oversold and fundamentally interesting, but patience over urgency — 12-month base case $120 (+25%), bull $180 if cloud, a 1260H reprieve, and a distillation-dispute resolution land; size for a $72 bear tail in a sanctions/IP-escalation scenario.
